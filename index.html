Why Security Matters

Every digital interaction implicitly trusts something. Security is the discipline of making that trust deliberate, measurable, and defensible.

At its core, security protects three things — the CIA Triad:

C
Confidentiality

Only the right people see the data. (Encryption, access control)

I
Integrity

Data isn't tampered with. (Hashing, signatures)

A
Availability

Systems are up when needed. (Backups, DDoS protection)

💰

Financial Impact

Average cost of a data breach in 2024: $4.88 million. A single compromised admin account can erase years of profit.

🏛️

Legal & Regulatory

GDPR, India's DPDP Act, HIPAA. Fines can hit 4% of global revenue. Engineers are increasingly liable.

🤝

Trust & Reputation

Users abandon platforms after breaches. 83% of consumers stop spending with a company for months post-incident.

🌐

National Security

Power grids, hospitals, telecom — all run on software. Cyber attacks are now acts of war.

Threats, Vulnerabilities & Risks

These three words get used interchangeably. They shouldn't be. Understanding the difference is the foundation of every security decision.

🎯 Threat

Who or what could harm you.

Example: A hacker, a disgruntled employee, malware, even a flood.

×

🩹 Vulnerability

The weakness they exploit.

Example: An unpatched server, a weak password, an open S3 bucket.

↓
= RISK

The probability and impact of the threat exploiting the vulnerability.

Real-world analogy: A burglar (threat) seeing an unlocked window (vulnerability) creates the risk of a break-in. Lock the window — no vulnerability — and the threat still exists but the risk drops near zero.

Password & Brute Force Attacks

A brute-force attack is the most primitive attack — try every possible combination until something works. Modern GPUs can guess billions of passwords per second. Let's see how your password holds up.

🎲

Dictionary Attack

Try common words and leaked passwords first. "password123" — cracked in microseconds.

🌈

Rainbow Tables

Precomputed hash → password lookups. Defeated by salting your hashes.

💧

Credential Stuffing

Take leaked username/password pairs from one site, try them everywhere. This works embarrassingly often.

🔑

Defenses

MFA, rate limiting, lockouts, password managers, passkeys. Length > complexity.

Security Breaches: Real Incidents

Theory is one thing. Watching billion-dollar companies fall is another. Here's a quick tour through the breaches that reshaped the industry.

2013 · Target

40 million credit cards stolen

Attackers got in through Target's HVAC vendor. Lesson: your security is only as strong as your weakest third party.

$292M loss · CEO fired read more →
2017 · Equifax

147 million identities exposed

An unpatched Apache Struts vulnerability sat for 2 months. SSNs, addresses, DOBs of nearly half of America leaked.

$1.4B settlement read more →
2017 · WannaCry

200,000+ machines across 150 countries

Ransomware exploited a leaked NSA tool (EternalBlue). The UK's NHS shut down hospitals. Real patients sent home.

$4B+ damage read more →
2020 · SolarWinds

18,000 organisations including US gov

A supply-chain attack — attackers poisoned a software update. Pentagon, Microsoft, FireEye all compromised.

Nation-state level read more →
2021 · Colonial Pipeline

2023 · MOVEit

Exposure & Attack Surface

Your attack surface is every point where an attacker could try to get in. Every API, every employee, every cloud service, every IoT device. Most companies don't even know how big theirs is.

Crown jewels you must protect Attack surface (entry points)
📈

Surface ↑ Risk ↑

Every new service, port, library, or endpoint expands what an attacker can target.

👻

Shadow IT

That side project on a dev's personal AWS? That forgotten subdomain? Attackers find them before you do.

🧹

Reduce, Don't Just Defend

Decommission what you don't use. Close unused ports. Less attack surface = less to defend.

🔭

Continuous Discovery

Your surface changes weekly. Tools like Shodan, ASM platforms, and bug bounties map it in real time.

Test Yourself

5-Question Sanity Check

Pick the best answer. Click to reveal explanations.

1. Which of these is a vulnerability (not a threat)?

A vulnerability is a weakness. Threats are the actors/events that exploit them. Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact.

2. Which password is hardest to brute-force?

Length crushes complexity. A 28-character passphrase takes trillions of years to crack vs. seconds for short "complex" passwords. (xkcd #936 was right.)

3. Colonial Pipeline (2021) was breached because of…

Yes — one password from a previous breach, reused on a legacy VPN account, no MFA. Shut down 45% of US East Coast fuel.

4. The CIA Triad stands for…

The three foundational goals every security control maps back to.

5. Best way to reduce attack surface?

Less surface = less to defend. Every endpoint you don't need is an endpoint that can't be attacked. This is called the principle of minimisation.
